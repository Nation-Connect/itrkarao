GST Registration :

All entities involved in buying or selling goods or providing services or both are required to register for GST,Under the new GST regime.

Once an entity crosses the minimum threshold turnover of starts a new business registration under GST is mandatory.

GST Turnover Limit

The GST turnover limit for regular GST registration for various service providers and goods supplier is provided below.

Service Providers

If the aggregate turnover of a person or entity is more than Rs.20 lakhs in a year is required to obtain GST registration.The GST turnover limit for special category states has been fixed at Rs.10 lakhs.

Goods Suppliers

Person who is exclusively engaged in the supply of goods with aggregate turnover crosses Rs.40  lakhs in a year is required to obtain GST registration. For this, the supplier must satisfy the following conditions:

  1. Providing no services.
  2. The supplier should not be engaged in making supplying goods within the same state supplies in the States of Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Puducherry, Sikkim, Telangana, Tripura and Uttarakhand.
  3. Should not be involved in ice cream, pan masala or tobacco supply.

If the above conditions fails, the supplier of goods would be required to obtain GST registration when the turnover crosses Rs.40 lakhs and for special category states its Rs.20 lakhs .

Special Category States:

Following states are listed as special category states, under GST- Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Jammu and Kashmir, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Himachal Pradesh and Uttarakhand.

Aggregate Turnover

Aggregate turnover is calculated based on the PAN. Hence, even if a person has business at multiple places, it must be summed to get the aggregate turnover.

Voluntary GST Registration

Irrespective of business turnover an entity or person can obtain GST registration at any-time. Hence, many businesses obtain GST registration in spite of not reaching the aggregate turnover limit. Reasons behind obtaining voluntary GST registration are as follows:

  1. Business credibility will improve
  2. To satisfy the requirements of B2B customers
  3. To claim input tax credit benefits

GST Registration Responsibilities

Registered entities have various responsibilities and compliance requirements from time to time. Failure to comply can lead to penalties and revocation by the authorities. Some of the main responsibilities of a person registered under GST are as follows:

  1. Collecting and remitting GST amount from customers
  2. Issuing GST invoice as per the GST rules and regulations
  3. Filing GST returns whenever even if there is no turnover or business activity based on turnover
  4. Filing annual GST return
  5. Maintaining all records for a period of 8 years pertaining to GST
